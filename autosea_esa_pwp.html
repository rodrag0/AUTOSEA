AUTOSEA

Space-Enabled Unmanned Surface Vessel Service for Maritime Surveillance and Subsea Infrastructure Monitoring

Delivering persistent, space-enabled USV surveillance for ports, security agencies, and offshore operators.

Objectives of the Service

[Image: LM450 USV in action]

AUTOSEA addresses maritime surveillance challenges: high costs of crewed surveys, diver risk for hull inspections, and infrequent subsea monitoring. The service provides autonomous USV operations with modular payloads (sonar, sensors) for bathymetric mapping, hull scanning, and persistent patrols. Key problems solved include limited endurance and manpower needs, delivering geo-stamped evidence via remote control.

Users and Their Needs

[Image: Port operations example]

Targeted users include port hydrography teams, coast guards, offshore operators, and researchers. Needs cover safe harbor operations, high-resolution bathymetry, non-diver hull inspections, continuous cable monitoring, and long-endurance data collection. Challenges include crew shortages, high operational costs, and reactive monitoring. Pilots/LOIs confirm demand, e.g., Valencia Port for hull scans.

Service/System Concept

[High-level architecture diagram]

AUTOSEA supplies geo-referenced data (sonar maps, anomaly alerts) via the LM450 USV (5.3m, hybrid propulsion, 350kg payload), DockMaster (autonomous docking/charging), and AutomatePro AI autonomy. The system follows: sensors → edge processing → SatCom upload → user dashboard. Platform: USV, infrastructure: docks, control: cloud. Enables 30+ day autonomous missions offshore.

Space Added Value

GNSS/RTK ensures cm-precision positioning critical for autonomous navigation offshore. Satellite communications (Starlink/Iridium) enable global remote operations and real-time data streaming. Combined, these assets deliver persistent coverage, reduce reliance on crewed vessels, and lower costs to €1.52/km versus €14/km for manned operations. Competitors lack integrated space-enabled autonomy for true persistence.
